# Strategy

## Working Name

East Meets Nash

## One-Sentence Promise

East Nashville, everything you wanted to know, nothing held back, and somehow still entertaining before coffee.

## Positioning

East Meets Nash is an East Nashville local media organization with the density of a neighborhood paper, the speed of a social feed, the nose of a gossip column, and the civic irritation level of someone trying to cross Gallatin at the wrong time.

The brand should feel local first, funny second, and AI-accelerated underneath. Readers do not need an AI explainer. They should just feel like the site is unnervingly fast, strangely funny, and always aware of what everyone is mad about.

## Audience

Primary readers are East Nashville residents:

- Long-time locals who know when a place has really changed.
- Newer residents who want inside baseball without feeling like tourists.
- Parents, restaurant people, musicians, civic watchers, renters, homeowners, and people who have strong opinions about cones, patios, parking, and brunch wait times.

Tourists can read it, but the product should not orient itself around them.

## Product Shape

The public web product should feel like a high-energy local newspaper:

- Homepage with main story, quick hits, categories, search, zone filters, events, restaurants, civic, gossip, and classifieds.
- Article pages optimized for search and local sharing.
- Sponsor placements that feel native but clearly marked.
- A morning brief and weekly flagship issue powered by beehiiv.

The logged-in product should feel more like an East Nashville social feed:

- Personalized beats and zones.
- Reactions, comments, upvotes, saves, and follows.
- ATXP balance, credits, purchases, classified posting, vouchers, tickets, and merch.
- A "your East Nashville" feed that learns what the reader clicks, reacts to, and complains about.

## Tone

Smart neighbor. Maximum side-eye. No swearing. Specific names when warranted. Never bland.

Mean is allowed only when it is funny, proportionate, and pointed at behavior, systems, public figures, businesses, or locally visible absurdity. If the joke depends on cruelty, punching down, body shaming, or inventing facts, cut it.

## Business Goals

- Launch publicly Thursday, May 14, 2026.
- Publish enough before launch that the site already feels alive.
- Reach $5,000 total revenue by December 31, 2026.
- Reach $20,000 monthly revenue within 12 months.

## Success Signals

- People forward stories because the headline is funny and the item is useful.
- Local businesses understand the sponsor offer without a sales call.
- Residents start sending tips, screenshots, corrections, complaints, and restaurant rumors.
- Search pages begin ranking for East Nashville restaurants, events, neighborhood issues, and civic topics.
- The AI reporter cast becomes a recognizable entertainment layer, not just bylines.
