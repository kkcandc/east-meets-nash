# Editorial Policy

## Editorial Mission

Cover East Nashville with speed, receipts, jokes, and a bias toward the stuff residents actually talk about.

## Story Labels

Every item gets one primary confidence label:

- `Confirmed` - backed by an official source, direct business post, public record, or named source.
- `Reported` - credible publication or reporter has it, but East Meets Nash has not independently confirmed it.
- `Group Chat Says` - sourced from Facebook, Nextdoor, Reddit, X, Instagram comments, reader tips, or community chatter.
- `Seen in the Wild` - observed from social posts, photos, signs, menus, flyers, permits, or screenshots.
- `Allegedly` - rumor or unverified claim with public interest. Must be framed carefully.
- `Tip Line` - anonymous or semi-anonymous reader-submitted item.
- `Correction` - correction, clarification, or update.

## Source Rules

- Every factual post must carry source links, source names, screenshots, embeds, or internal source notes.
- Every published story should ship with a media pack: real location/source photo when available, hero image/custom art only as fallback, source screenshot/capture or public embed when useful, source trail as links, map/embed when useful, and social cuts. Inline images must be story-specific, not generic filler; food/menu shots belong only when the food/menu is part of the reported angle. Do not publish source receipt cards as article images.
- Facebook and Nextdoor items can be published when framed as community chatter, not automatically treated as fact.
- Do not publish accusations of crimes, scams, harassment, abuse, or misconduct unless sourced to a public record, official statement, named source willing to stand behind it, or multiple credible sources.
- If a source contradicts another source, say so plainly.
- Private citizens can be named when the story has public interest, but avoid mugshots and personal details unless there is a strong reason.

## Humor Rules

Allowed:

- Teasing East Nashville habits, patterns, businesses, public officials, development drama, traffic, tourists, and hyperlocal absurdity.
- Character-driven commentary from AI reporters.
- Specific, funny, sourced criticism.

Avoid:

- Swearing.
- Body shaming.
- Jokes based on protected classes.
- Invented facts.
- Meanness that is not funny.
- Humiliation of private people when the public value is low.

## Rumor and Gossip

Rumor is part of the product, but it must be aggressively labeled. A rumor post should usually answer:

- What are people saying?
- Where did it come from?
- What do we know?
- What do we not know?
- What would confirm or disprove it?

## Corrections and Takedowns

- Corrections should be fast, visible, and a little human.
- Takedown requests should trigger a quick review by Kenny or the operating agent.
- If a post is wrong, update the post rather than quietly deleting it unless deletion is clearly the cleaner fix.
- Keep an internal audit note with the original source, publish time, change time, and reason.

## Auto-Publish Default

Routine items can auto-publish. Escalate before publishing only when:

- The item alleges serious misconduct by a private citizen.
- The system cannot identify a source trail.
- The item uses a private screenshot with sensitive personal information.
- The likely downside is much larger than the neighborhood value.
