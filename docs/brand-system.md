# Brand System

## Brand Sentence

East Meets Nash is the East Nashville paper that behaves like the group text got a press pass.

## Voice

- Insider, never tourist.
- Funny, but useful first.
- Specific, not vague.
- No swearing.
- Tease the neighborhood like someone who lives here and still loves it.
- Use names when the source trail supports it.
- Let the reporter cast have opinions.

## House Lines

- East Nashville, with receipts.
- The group text got a press pass.
- Patios, permits, traffic, gossip.
- Everything east of the river that is fit to print and several things that are only fit to whisper.
- Local news for people who know which Kroger mood they are in.

## Visual Direction

The look should feel like a civic institution that found a confetti cannon in a council chamber.

- Parody civic seal.
- Heavy serif headlines.
- Thick black rules.
- Paper background.
- Tomato red, hot-chicken gold, park green, river blue, and a small amount of nightclub plum.
- Polished enough to trust, scrappy enough to share.

## Logo Direction

Primary mark:

- Circular parody civic seal.
- `EMN` monogram first, with skyline/river/local-color hints around it.
- No long text wrapped around the seal; the surrounding page copy can carry the joke.
- Tiny official nonsense is allowed only when it does not hurt readability.

Secondary marks:

- Ultra-simple `EMN` monogram.
- Reporter headshot lockups.
- "Group Chat Says" stamp.
- "Cone Watch" stamp.
- "Side-Eye" reaction badge.

## Editorial Franchises

- What People Are Mad About This Week
- The Sidewalk Parent Index
- Permit File Says
- Patio Court
- Gallatin Weather Report
- Soft Launch Watch
- Five Points FOMO
- The East Nash Receipt Drawer
- Weekend Chaos Calendar
- Bless This Listing

## Reaction Language

- ❤️ Love - the normal positive reaction.
- 👀 Side-Eye - suspicious.
- Keep Hot Chicken, Cone Watch, Porch Clap, Bless It, and Five Points FOMO as editorial flavor or limited-run stamps, not the default reaction set.

## Do Not Sound Like

- A tourism board.
- A real estate brochure.
- A generic local newsletter.
- A sanctimonious civic nonprofit.
- A national media brand discovering neighborhoods exist.
